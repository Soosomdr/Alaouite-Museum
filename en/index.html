Idris I

Idris I, founder of the Idrisid dynasty, is regarded as the founding father of Morocco. Youssef Ben Tachfine

Youssef Ben Tachfine was the founder of the Almoravid dynasty. He united the Berber tribes of the Sahara and established the Almoravid empire in the 11th century. His leadership led to the expansion of the empire, which included territories in both North Africa and southern Spain, becoming one of the most powerful dynasties in medieval history.

Ahmad Al Mansur

Ahmad Al Mansur was the Sultan of the Saadi dynasty in Morocco during the late 16th century. His reign is noted for significant military victories, including the Battle of the Three Kings, and for establishing Morocco as a major regional power. He also promoted economic development and cultural growth, leaving a lasting impact on Moroccan history.

Mohammed V

Mohammed V was the Sultan and later King of Morocco in the mid-20th century. He played a crucial role in the country's independence from French and Spanish colonial rule. His leadership during the nationalist movement and his subsequent reign as king were pivotal in shaping modern Morocco.
